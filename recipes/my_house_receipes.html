Rwandan Matoke Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 green unripe bananas (matoke)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt to taste
  • Water

Instructions:

  1. Peel the green bananas (matoke) and cut them into chunks.
  2. In a large pot, add water and bring it to a boil. Add the banana chunks and cook for about 10 minutes or until they are tender.
  3. Drain the cooked bananas and set them aside.
  4. In a separate pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  5. Add the chopped onions and minced garlic to the pan. Sauté until the onions are translucent.
  6. Add the diced tomatoes, green bell pepper, and red bell pepper to the pan. Stir and cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.
  7. Add the cooked banana chunks to the pan and mix them with the sautéed vegetables.
  8. Season with curry powder, paprika, turmeric, and salt. Stir well to coat the bananas and vegetables with the spices.
  9. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the matoke simmer for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  10. Remove from heat and serve the Rwandan matoke while hot.
